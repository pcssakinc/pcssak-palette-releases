# Why PCssak Palette Exists

[한국어](PROJECT-STORY.ko.md)

## A color that was difficult to trust

PCssak Palette began with a personal limitation, not a feature checklist. Its founder is a
Korean solo developer with a color-vision deficiency that makes cyan and green difficult to
distinguish. Finding the same color again, judging whether two colors were truly different, or
choosing status colors could require guesswork or another person's confirmation.

The first idea was a small tool that could find and compare colors more reliably. It grew into a
larger question:

> Can a person with color-vision differences build a professional color system using objective
> evidence, without claiming that software knows what is beautiful?

PCssak Palette therefore focuses on reproducible questions: contrast ratios, simulated
distinguishability, role conflicts, deterministic corrections, and stable delivery formats.
Taste, culture, brand meaning, and the final design decision remain human work.

## How it developed

This timeline is the founder's record of the tools used. It does not imply sponsorship,
endorsement, or transferred responsibility.

- **January 2026:** Learning and prototyping began with Antigravity while several PC utility
  ideas were turned into product plans.
- **Planning phase:** Claude Opus 4.5 assisted with early product structure and requirements.
- **Current phase:** OpenAI Codex assists with implementation review, regression work, release
  engineering, and documentation.

AI accelerated the work, but the founder remains the product owner: defining the problem,
accepting tradeoffs, controlling credentials and releases, and maintaining what users receive.
The project publishes its [AI development policy](AI-DEVELOPMENT-POLICY.md) because honesty about
the process matters more than presenting AI as a magic quality label.

## What PCSSAK wants to earn

PCSSAK is building a family of focused Windows tools. Trust should come from observable behavior:

- clear product boundaries and known limitations;
- local processing and narrow permissions where practical;
- tests and deterministic calculations for objective claims;
- reviewed dependencies and bundled license notices;
- signed update packages, published hashes, and reproducible release records;
- honest bug handling without hiding behind the Early Access label.

PCssak Palette is the first public product. The long-term goal is not to look like a large company.
It is to show that a small Korean software project can be careful, understandable, and worthy of
continued trust.
